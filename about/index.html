About Damon Hoody (aka tox)

I’m Damon Hoody — a network engineer in training with a strong foundation in systems, routing, switching, and network security. I currently work as a Computer Technician at Cuyahoga Valley Career Center, wherein I recently graduated with honors and distinction at the Cisco Networking Academy. I am currently preparing to pursue my B.S. in Network Engineering and Security from Western Governors University.

My technical work spans a range of infrastructure and operations roles: I’ve led lab-wide Windows system deployments, configured core switching for 10Gbps backup uplinks, and engineered redundant fiber connectivity across multiple MDF/IDF closets. I’ve supported real-world migration projects (including Hyper-V and Synology NAS provisioning), as well as implemented enterprise tools like Freshdesk, NetAlly, and ConnectWise.

Outside of work, I run a hobbyist homelab that reflects my commitment to practical experience. My lab stack includes a Cisco 3560x, Ubiquiti USG Pro, multiple APs, mail and file servers, with core routing, VPNs, and network services simulated via GNS3. I also maintain a secure mail server (@hoody.cx) with DNS standards like SPF, DKIM, and DMARC fully implemented.

I currently hold my CCNA, Network+, Security+, and A+, certifications, as well as CCST & CC.

This blog is where I document my lessons learned as a young engineer: from switch provisioning and Layer 2 design, to building WAN topologies and securing edge devices. While my focus has shifted away from capture-the-flag exercises and offensive security, my dedication to secure, resilient networks remains front and center.

Feel free to explore the site. If you work in network operations or infrastructure security — or you’re also learning the ropes — there will be something here for you, either now or in the near future.

God bless you.
